tradeApp vs India Institutional Options Selling — Deep Research Comparison
Context
This is a research and analysis document — not a code change plan. The user wants a comprehensive, honest comparison of the tradeApp options strategy against the best institutional options selling systems operating in India (NSE F&O market). The goal is to identify gaps, strengths, alignments, and upgrade opportunities.

1. OVERVIEW OF BOTH SYSTEMS
tradeApp Strategy (VRP Premium Harvester)
A Flask-based systematic options selling system for NSE F&O. Built around Volatility Risk Premium (VRP) harvesting — selling options when IV significantly exceeds Realized Volatility. Covers 107 symbols (4 indices + 103 NIFTY100 stocks).

India's #1 Institutional Standard
Dominant approach: Delta-neutral, systematic short volatility run by global HFT/prop firms (Optiver, Citadel, Jane Street, IMC, Jump Trading) and domestic prop desks (Graviton Research Capital, Quadeye, Dolat Capital). Also captured in Category III AIFs (Alpha Alternatives MSAR: 12.6% CAGR, Sharpe 3.0).

The defining institutional characteristic: continuous delta-hedging with futures to isolate pure volatility risk premium — not directional theta decay.

4. TOP 5 UPGRADE PRIORITIES (TO CLOSE INSTITUTIONAL GAP)
Priority 1 — Futures Delta Hedging (CRITICAL)
What: Add intraday delta neutralization via Nifty/BankNifty futures alongside current option rolls. Why: This is the single feature that separates institutional vol sellers from retail theta farmers. Without it, P&L mixes directional risk with pure vol risk. How: In engine.py DynamicHedgingEngine, calculate net portfolio delta and auto-generate a paired futures order when delta exceeds ±0.15. Update modules/broker.py to handle futures leg.

Priority 2 — Event Calendar Override (HIGH)
What: Block new entries and optionally close/hedge existing positions around major scheduled events (RBI MPC, Union Budget, FOMC, election results, quarterly results). Why: IV-crush plays earn ~40-60% of annual premium in event weeks — but unprotected shorts during unexpected outcomes (e.g., election surprise) are the #1 cause of blowup. How: Add EVENT_CALENDAR in config.py with RBI/FOMC/Budget dates. In scanner.py scan_metrics(), check if next 3 calendar days contain an event and suppress signals.

Priority 3 — SEBI 2024 Regulatory Audit (HIGH)
What: Audit config.py for: BANKNIFTY weekly removal, NIFTY 50 lot size change (25→75), new ELM surcharge (2% short + 2% expiry day), calendar spread margin changes. Why: Using stale lot sizes gives wrong position sizing and wrong P&L. Entering BANKNIFTY weekly trades that no longer exist fails silently. How: Update SYMBOL_LOT_SIZES, remove weekly BANKNIFTY/FINNIFTY entries from AUTO_SCAN_SYMBOLS weekly slot, update RISK_CFG margin buffer to account for 4% effective ELM (2% standard + 2% expiry-day).

Priority 4 — Basket/Combo Order Execution (MEDIUM)
What: Replace sequential leg execution with Zerodha basket orders for iron condors (all 4 legs simultaneously). Why: Leg risk from sequential fills costs 0.5-1% per position in adverse markets. Zerodha Kite API supports basket_orders endpoint. How: In modules/broker.py, add place_basket_order() wrapper using Kite's multi-leg execution. Modify engine.py position entry to use basket for IC/spreads.

Priority 5 — Margin Utilization Tighten (MEDIUM)
What: Reduce max_margin_utilization_pct from 80% → 60% in config.py. Why: Post-SEBI surcharges mean a fully loaded 25-position portfolio at 80% utilization has minimal buffer for simultaneous adverse moves. At 60%, there's 20% headroom to absorb intraday margin calls before forced liquidation. How: Single config change: config.py RISK_CFG → max_margin_pct: 0.60.

5. WHAT tradeApp DOES BETTER THAN MOST INSTITUTIONAL SYSTEMS
Multi-symbol stock coverage: Most institutional desks are index-only. tradeApp's 103-stock scan with sector caps and correlation constraints is genuinely differentiated. Stock IV tends to be richer (higher IV-RV spreads) than index IV — but only if liquidity and bid-ask are managed.

3-condition VRP gate: The simultaneous IV-RV spread + IV Percentile + IV/RV Ratio gate is more conservative and rigorous than typical systematic funds that use only IV Rank.

Skew-adaptive structure selection: Switching from IC → put calendar → put spread → ratio call spread based on real-time skew ratios is institutional desk behavior — not seen in retail systematic strategies.

Volga/Vanna corrections: The Vanna exit (IV drops 20% + spot within 0.5%) and Volga stress correction (+15% for VIX doubling) reflect second-order Greeks awareness rare in non-HFT systems.

Circuit breaker with gate tightening: The monthly drawdown circuit breaker automatically tightening the entry gate (not just stopping new entries) is a thoughtful risk-management design.
